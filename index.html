Mission

Two pathways. One purpose.

Passd Down is a student-led initiative built to keep clothing useful for as long as possible. Wearable clothes are donated to people in need, while damaged or unwearable clothes are separated and recycled into new yarn.

Damaged Clothes

Why We Started

Clothes shouldn't end in landfills.

Wearable items can support people and communities that need quality clothing.
Damaged textiles can be recycled into yarn instead of being sent to landfills.

What You Can Donate

We accept both wearable and damaged clothing. Wearable clothes are donated, and damaged clothes are recycled into yarn.

Accepted

  • Shirts
  • T-shirts
  • Jeans
  • Jackets
  • Hoodies
  • School Uniforms
  • Blankets
  • Shoes
  • Bags
  • Damaged clothing for recycling
  • Clean textile scraps

Not Accepted

  • Undergarments
  • Wet clothes
  • Moldy items
  • Contaminated textiles
  • Heavily soiled footwear

FAQ

Quick answers before you donate.

Students, families, neighbors, and community members can donate wearable or damaged clothing.

We accept both wearable and damaged clothing. Wearable clothes are donated, while damaged clothing is separated for textile recycling into yarn.

Use the donation form and the team will coordinate collection details with you.

Wearable donations are redistributed to people and communities that can benefit from them. Damaged textiles are separated for recycling.

Instead of ending up in landfills, damaged clothing is separated and recycled into yarn, giving the material a second life.
